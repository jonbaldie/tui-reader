# Chapter 1: The Beginning

Every great story has a beginning. This is ours. In the beginning, there
was nothing but a blank terminal and a blinking cursor. Then someone had
an idea: what if we could read books right here, in the terminal?

And so the TUI reader was born. Not from necessity, but from the simple
joy of reading text in a monospace font, surrounded by nothing but the
quiet hum of a computer.

The first chapter is often the hardest to write, but the easiest to read.
It sets the stage, introduces the characters, and promises adventures to
come. We shall not disappoint.

Consider the raven, who knows nothing of books or terminals, yet lives a
perfectly content existence. Perhaps there is wisdom in simplicity, in
reading words as they appear, one page at a time, without the distraction
of flashy graphics or animated page turns.

This reader does one thing, and it does it well: it lets you read.

# Chapter 2: The Middle

The middle of any story is where the real work happens. Characters develop,
plots thicken, and the reader finds themselves pulled deeper into the
narrative.

In our case, the middle is about pagination. How do you take a continuous
stream of text and break it into discrete, readable pages? The answer is
surprisingly simple: count lines, respect word boundaries, and trust the
reader to press the right arrow key when they're ready.

We also handle links here. See, in a book, you might have a table of
contents that points to specific chapters. In our reader, those links are
real and navigable. Press tab to highlight them, enter to follow them,
and 'b' to return to where you were.

It's a simple system, but an effective one. No configuration needed, no
settings to tweak. We made all the decisions so you don't have to.

The philosophy is borrowed from the best e-readers: get out of the way
and let the words speak for themselves. The frame should enhance the
reading experience, not dominate it.

# Chapter 3: The End

All good things must come to an end, and so must this sample document.
We hope you've enjoyed this brief tour of the TUI reader.

To summarize what you've learned:

1. Arrow keys (or h/l) navigate between pages
2. Home/End (or g/G) jump to the first/last page
3. Tab cycles through links on the current page
4. Enter follows a selected link
5. 'b' goes back to where you came from
6. 'q' or Esc quits the reader

That's it. No menus, no preferences, no complexity. Just reading.

Thank you for reading. Now go read something wonderful.
